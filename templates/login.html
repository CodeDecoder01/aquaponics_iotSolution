What is Aquaponics?



Aquaponics is the interlinking of hydroponics with aquaculture.
Aquatic animals like fishes are bred in a tank and the water they are bred in is allowed to collect the nitrogen wastes excreted by them. This water is circulated to another tank with plants whose roots absorb nutrients through the water.

The water that is now clear is recirculated back to the former tank.A closed loop is formed which helps in less usage of water whilst obtaining the right nutrients for organic plant growth.There is no requirement of soil in the system and hence devoid of fertilizers and pesticides usage.











Advantages of Aquaponics over conventional Agriculture



Water conservation



Yield per square feet is high



Devoid of fertilizers and pesticides.



Not seasonal in nature



No requirement of soil
